### **Travel Agency Financial Performance Dashboard: Regional Pricing, Revenue Trends, and Profitability Analysis**

---

### **1. Project Overview**
**Project Title:** Travel Agency Sales & Revenue Performance Dashboard
**Description:** This Excel-based dashboard provides a high-level overview of a travel agency's financial and operational performance. It visualizes key metrics such as total revenue (6.77M), customer base size (1.34K), and total sales quantity (13K). The dashboard breaks down pricing structures by geographic region, analyzes revenue contributions across different product categories and sub-categories, and drills down into profit generation at both the U.S. state and regional levels.

---

### **2. Objective**
The primary objective of this dashboard is to equip travel agency management with a clear, visual understanding of where their revenue and profit are coming from. Specific goals include:
*   **Pricing Strategy Assessment:** To compare average unit pricing across different U.S. regions (East, North, South, West).
*   **Product Mix Analysis:** To evaluate how different product categories (A, B, C, D) and their sub-categories (Type 1, 2, 3) are generating revenue.
*   **Geographic Profitability Mapping:** To pinpoint the most profitable U.S. states and broader regions to guide sales and marketing focus.
*   **Operational Scale Tracking:** To monitor the total customer base and the volume of services rendered (Quantity).

---

### **3. Dashboard Features**
*   **Executive Summary KPI Tabs (Top Right):** Three pill-shaped cards displaying top-level business metrics: *Sum of Revenue (6.77M)*, *Count of Customer (1,341)*, and *Sum of Quantity (13K)*.
*   **Regional Pricing Analysis (Top Left):** A vertical bar chart showing *Region wise Unit Price*. It highlights that the "North" region commands the highest average price, while the "South" has the lowest.
*   **3D Revenue Trend Chart (Top Center):** A 3D Surface/Line chart illustrating *Revenue based on Category & Sub-Category*. It plots revenue across four categories (A, B, C, D), with separate colored lines tracking the three different product types (Type1, Type2, Type3).
*   **State-Level Profit Breakdown (Bottom Left):** A horizontal bar chart showing *State wise Profit* for various U.S. states. It ranks states from top (West Virginia) to bottom (Arkansas, though Arkansas is also highly profitable) by total profit generated.
*   **Regional Profit Radar (Bottom Right):** A radar (spider) chart displaying *Region wise Profit*. It compares the four major regions, showing a roughly balanced distribution with the East slightly ahead.

---

### **4. Business Questions**
This dashboard is designed to answer the following critical business questions:
1.  Which U.S. region is charging the highest prices for travel services, and is this justified by higher demand or exclusivity?
2.  How does revenue fluctuate across our four main product categories, and which specific product type within those categories is the strongest seller?
3.  Which specific U.S. states are the absolute profit leaders, and which regions consistently generate the most total profit?
4.  What is the overall scale of our customer base and service volume (quantity)?

---

### **5. Key Insights (Data-Driven)**
*   **The North is the Premium Market:** The bar chart shows the *North* region has a significantly higher unit price (181,761.52) compared to the South (165,206.31). This suggests a premium market or higher-cost services in the North.
*   **Category D Experiences a Revenue Dip:** The 3D line chart reveals a clear pattern. While revenue for Type2 and Type3 is relatively stable across categories A, B, and C, there is a distinct **downward dip in revenue for all product types when reaching Category D**. This indicates Category D products or services are underperforming.
*   **Florida is the Uncontested Profit Champion:** The state-wise bar chart shows **Florida** generating a massive **$115,840.24** in profit, far surpassing the second-highest state, Colorado ($95,989.38). This highlights Florida as the critical market for the agency.
*   **Balanced Regional Profitability:** Despite the unit price differences in the top chart, the Radar chart at the bottom right shows that total profit is actually quite evenly distributed across the four regions (East, North, South, West), all hovering in the 75K to 89K range. The East (89K) is the slight leader.
*   **Stable Customer Reach:** The KPI cards indicate a broad operational scale with over 1.3K customers and 13K units sold, generating nearly $6.8 million in revenue.

### **8. Business Value**
*   **Strategic Market Focus (Florida):** The dashboard clearly identifies Florida as the primary profit engine. Leadership can use this data to allocate the bulk of their marketing budget toward Florida-based promotions and partnerships. Conversely, they can investigate lower-performing states to see if operational costs are too high.
*   **Re-evaluating Category D:** The 3D revenue chart provides a clear warning signal. Category D is losing momentum across all three types. The company needs to analyze whether the services in Category D are outdated, overpriced relative to their value, or poorly marketed, and take immediate corrective action.
*   **Pricing Model Adjustment:** The high unit price in the North region justifies a premium marketing approach there. However, the agency can look at the South region (lower unit price but comparable regional profit) to see if lowering prices slightly in the North could drive higher quantity and potentially offset the unit price drop to grow total revenue.
*   **Sales Management:** State-level profit data allows for highly targeted sales team management. The regional manager can rank their sales reps based on performance in these specific states, rewarding those who drive profit in top-tier states like Florida and Colorado.
